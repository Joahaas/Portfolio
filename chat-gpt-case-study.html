CHAT GPT_BIG PROMPTS?
NO PROBLEM

Developing a new feature for Chat GPT to help users navigate big prompts and organize their conversations better.

Project overview

Before we
get started

Timeline: 3 weeks project - 2025
Tools: Figma, Chat GPT, Adobe & Synthetic Users
Role: Product Designer

Description: We were given a specific challenge to build a new feature for a already well established Digital Platform. While I was researching and in the process of brainstorming ideas with Chat GPT, I struggled to keep track of the specific ideas in tokens (responses) and also struggled to navigate through the prompt that was getting very big.

At the same time I was also almost settling on a specific new feature for Pinterest. Then I suddenly realized "what if we put 1 + 1 together and get the best of both worlds Pinterest and Chat GPT?" So I pitched the idea of adding bookmarking, saving and organizing ideas and specific sections of tokens for Chat GPT to my group.

Everybody loved the idea. 👌 Now we had to actually see if other users agreed 🤔

While this prototype wasn't user-tested formally, the feature set is designed to address clear usability pain points — with success metrics like..

Easier information management
Faster navigation in long prompts
Reduced friction in reusing ideas
Longer user retention

The Problem

Getting a little bit tense 😅

The problem: Disorganization and inefficiency when working with long prompts in ChatGPT.

Users often struggle to navigate lengthy conversations, reuse key snippets, and maintain continuity between sessions. ChatGPT lacks built-in tools to help users structure, highlight, or return to important parts of a conversation — leading to wasted time, confusion, and repeated effort.

before

after

Who was affected: 🎯 Power users, students, professionals, and creatives who rely on ChatGPT for research, writing, ideation, or coding — often using long prompts and complex conversations.
Everyone who is using ChatGPT more collaboratively.

These users often faced:

  • Difficulty finding important parts of past chats
  • Frustration when losing track of ideas
  • Wasted time rewriting or searching for content
  • Lack of continuity between sessions

Why was this important: ...because 🌱 ChatGPT lacked proper organization tools, making it hard to manage long prompts and retrieve valuable information later.

What opportunity we saw:

to transform ChatGPT from a one-off interaction tool into a long-term productivity companion.

We noticed that many users were already treating ChatGPT like a workspace — brainstorming, coding, writing — but lacked the tools to organize, revisit, and build on their work.

By introducing features like saved snippets, a navigation bar for long prompts, and a snippet library, we saw the chance to:

  • Improve workflow continuity
  • Reduce cognitive load
  • Make the app feel more like a creative environment than a chatbot

This was a clear opportunity to bridge the gap between conversation and creation.

Competitors outperform ChatGPT in context window size.

But now your are asking what exactly is a context window?

In AI platforms like ChatGPT, the context window refers to the amount of text (in tokens) that the model can "remember" and process at one time. It's essentially the model's short-term memory.

Imagine you're having a conversation with someone who can only recall the last few pages of your chat — that's the context window in action. The model uses it to understand the ongoing discussion, reference past messages, and generate coherent responses.

Why Context Windows Matter:

  • Better summaries of large texts
  • Improved complex problem solving across longer tasks
  • Less fragmentation of text (fewer input limitations)

Field Research

We used Synthetic users (AI platform that helps to conduct interviews and user research using AI) and real field interviews with real users to understand their pain points and priorities.

We found 6 primary user pain points:

ChatGPT's outputs can't be trusted due to occasional inaccuracies
information sources often feel 'sketchy'
users rely on external tools to provide queries some structure, which requires significant upfront effort
organising ChatGPT responses is hard and leads to inefficiencies and frustration
it's difficult to find specific responses
saved responses often loose their original context, requiring additional time to re-interprete that content

7 takeaways:

losing track of important responses triggers anxiety and frustration, disrupting workflows and reducing trust
saving and reviewing responses helps shape the users view on a specific topic
users prefer to save snippets instead of entire conversations, reducing information overload
users want the ability to save and add notes to content, preserving context and intended use
advanced users build local knowledge bases by saving their ChatGPT responses into categorized repositories
depending on the user's profile, they might wish to filter like data ranges or response types
users want the ability to tag and categorize responses directly with ChatGPT

2 surprising infos:

ChatGPT can be a catalyst for critical thinking and idea generation
revisiting saved responses frequently sparks new connections

Ideation | Brainstorming

Ideas, meet Post-its.
Post-its, meet chaos.

Based on the information we had above we did some HOW MIGHT WE'S in order to better define our problem statement. Then we did a prioritization matrix to figure out the value and feasibility of each hmw - we ended up focusing more on two trying to connect the other hmws when and where we could:

How might we make it easy for users to highlight and save specific sections of content for quick reference?

How might we design a virtual pinboard that feels intuitive and adapts to individual organization styles?

How might we allow users to categorize saved content with custom color-coding to reflect their unique priorities?

How might we remind users to revisit saved content without overwhelming them with notifications?

How might we simplify the process of bookmarking responses with categories to suit different personal workflows?

How might we allow users to attach personal notes to saved content for added context or future reference?

This helped us formulate our final problem statement:

"Users face challenges in saving, organizing, and retrieving valuable content in ChatGPT, which disrupts workflows and adds unnecessary cognitive strain. The lack of essential tools — such as highlighting, categorization, and annotations — leaves critical insights scattered and hard to manage."

Then we had a brainstorming section and then we made another prioritization matrix together with a MOSCOW in order to further narrow our ideas and efforts.

The Final Design

Designeeeers...assemble!

We presented a redesigned ChatGPT interface that allows users to save, organize, and revisit key responses using features like snippet bookmarking, categorized folders, a quick-access bar, and note attachments. These tools were seamlessly integrated into the existing UI to enhance usability without overwhelming the user.

Navigation Bar

The AI could change the links and titles of the navigation bar as the conversation was ongoing

Highlighting option

Pop-up where the user can highlight specific parts of the prompts, copy, and inspect the information

Quick Access Bar

Opens up a side bar that has all the saved snippets where you can add comments and other customizable options

Library

Overview of all saved snippets organized by categories and tags

Option to choose snippets and rewind them into new conversations to get surprising results from the AI

Why is this the best version?

This version directly addresses user pain points such as:

  • Losing important responses
  • Information overload
  • Lack of easy content retrieval

It also supports different workflows (creative, research, teaching, etc.) by introducing:

  • Snippet-based saving
  • Custom tags and folders
  • Smart reminders without pushy notifications
  • Context-preserving notes
  • Quick access to recent and most-used saves.

User expectations around personalization and clarity were core to our design decisions.
