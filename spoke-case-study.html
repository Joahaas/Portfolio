Overview

What was this project about

Spoke is a conversation-first language learning app designed to help users practice speaking through real-life scenarios.
Unlike traditional apps focused on vocabulary and grammar drills, Spoke emphasizes natural, confidence-building interactions with an AI partner.

Timeline2 months
ToolsFigma, Figjam & Synthetic Users
Team3 Designers
My roleUI Design

Problem statement

Most language learners can read and understand words, but freeze up when it's time to speak — because traditional apps focus on memorization, not real conversation scenarios

Relevant Insights

Our three main competitors taught us which features we should take into account when planning the information architecture of our platform.

Duolingo

Key learnings from Duolingo

  • Gamification elements like streaks, badges, and progress indicators that build habit and motivation
  • Micro-interactions and clear visual feedback that make the experience feel rewarding and intuitive
  • A scalable yet personal interface that adapts well to different user journeys while staying consistent
Opportunity for improvement

Limited depth for advanced users – Duolingo is highly effective for beginners, but its structure and content often lack depth for intermediate or advanced users. This highlights the opportunity to design more adaptive, layered experiences that grow with the user.

Speak

Key learnings from Speak

  • Conversational UI Speak uses simulated real-life dialogues, helping users feel like they're interacting with a person rather than just completing a task.
  • Voice-first designThe app is optimized for speaking out loud, not typing or tapping, reinforcing the core learning behavior.
  • AI-powered correctionsUsers receive specific, real-time feedback on pronunciation and sentence structure.
Opportunity for improvement

Rigid conversation structure – While Speak offers guided dialogues, interactions often feel scripted and predictable. There's room to introduce more open-ended responses or branching scenarios, allowing users to practice more natural, unscripted communication — especially for real-world situations.

Elsa

Key learnings from Elsa

  • Voice-focused interactions create a more natural and immersive learning experience
  • Real-time feedback on pronunciation builds user confidence and encourages self-correction
  • Personalized learning paths based on skill level and goals make the experience feel tailored
Opportunity for improvement

Limited emotional connection – While ELSA provides useful pronunciation feedback, the experience can feel robotic or transactional, lacking the warmth or personal storytelling that helps users stay emotionally engaged over time.

Key insight

Across all three apps we found that the interaction with AI voices was very robotic and mechanical. Not focused on real-life scenarios or about talking and connecting with other real people. Humans talk to each other to connect and learn from one another. That should be the focus of our app.

Key insight

Users aren't just looking to improve their language skills — they're seeking a safe, social space where they can connect with other learners, feel free to make mistakes, and grow through authentic, low-pressure conversations.

Design Critique with OLX Designers

We presented Spoke to the design team at OLX Portugal as part of a live critique session. The goal was to gather constructive feedback from experienced product designers and test the clarity, relevance, and usability of our concept in a professional setting.

Key learning from the Design Critique

  • The OLX team appreciated our focus on a minimalist interface, encouraging us to simplify even further and remove distractions from the core user flow.
  • They pushed us to revisit the Jobs-to-be-Done and ensure every feature clearly served the user's underlying goals
  • A major point of feedback was the importance of a gentle, supportive onboarding, especially for users who may feel self-conscious or overwhelmed in a new voice-based platform.
